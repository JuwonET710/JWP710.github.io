Yellowstone
Yellowstone was the first national park in the U.S. and is also widely held to be the first national park in the world.
The park is known for its wildlife and its many geothermal features, especially Old Faithful geyser, one of its most popular.

Yosemite
Yosemite is one of the largest and least fragmented habitat blocks in the Sierra Nevada, and the park supports a diversity of plants and animals.
The park has an elevation range from 2,127 to 13,114 feet and contains five major vegetation zones: chaparral and oak woodland.

Grand Canyon
The Grand Canyon is known for its visually overwhelming size and its intricate and colorful landscape.
Geologically, it is significant because of the thick sequence of ancient rocks that are well preserved and exposed in the walls of the canyon.
